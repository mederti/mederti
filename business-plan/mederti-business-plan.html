Executive Summary

A real, growing, expensive problem — with no system of record.

Drug shortages have roughly tripled over the past decade. Every developed health system now runs a permanent shortage desk, yet the underlying data lives in dozens of incompatible national registries, PDFs and email alerts. No one owns the global picture. Mederti does.

The problem

A hospital pharmacist learning that a critical injectable is short must today check the FDA, their national regulator, wholesaler portals and manufacturer notices — each in a different format, none cross-referenced, none predictive. The answer to "what do I do about it" doesn't exist in one place.

The solution

Mederti ingests every major regulator daily, resolves drugs to a canonical identity across brands, salts and countries, and layers Claude-level synthesis on top. One search returns the global shortage status, alternatives, recalls and an operational answer — grounded in the database, not hallucinated.

Why now

Three forces converge: shortages at record highs and politically salient; regulators mandating supply-chain transparency (EU, US, UK); and frontier LLMs finally able to synthesise messy regulatory text reliably. The data was always there — the means to unify and reason over it arrived in 2024–25.

Where we are

Not a deck — a live product. 29,000+ shortage events, 20+ regulators on daily cron, persona-aware UX for pharmacists, procurement and suppliers, and a tool-using Claude chat grounded in the database. Built and shipping.

01 — The Problem

The data exists. The intelligence doesn't.

Drug shortages are a structural feature of modern pharmaceutical supply chains — and the people managing them are flying blind across a dozen disconnected sources.

Fragmented

Every country runs its own shortage registry — FDA, TGA, EMA, MHRA, Health Canada, plus 15+ others — in different schemas, languages and update cadences. None talk to each other.

Unstructured

The same molecule appears as a brand, a salt form, an INN and a local trade name. "Is amoxicillin short?" has no single answer because the data was never resolved to one entity.

Backward-looking

Registries tell you a drug is short. None tell you what to do, what the alternatives are, or which drug is about to go short next.

Who feels the pain — and what it costs them

PersonaToday's workflowCost of getting it wrong
Hospital pharmacistManually checks 4–6 portals per drug; rebuilds substitution plans by handHours/day of clinical time; delayed or unsafe substitutions
Procurement / supply chainReacts to stockouts after they happen; no forward signalEmergency spot-buys at 5–50× price; tendering chaos
Suppliers & manufacturersNo visibility into where demand gaps are opening globallyMissed revenue; mis-allocated production
Regulators & governmentOwn national data only; no cross-border early warningPublic-health exposure; political risk
"There is no Bloomberg Terminal for drug supply. Every stakeholder is reconstructing the same global picture, badly, every single day."

This is not a niche inconvenience. The U.S. alone consistently tracks 250–300+ active national shortages at any time; the EU and individual member states track hundreds more. Managing them is a permanent, staffed, expensive function at every major health system on earth — and the tooling is spreadsheets and email.

02 — The Solution

One source of truth, with Claude on top.

Mederti unifies global regulatory data into a canonical drug graph, then makes it answerable in plain language — grounded in the database, never invented.

1 · Ingest everything, daily

68 automated scrapers pull shortage and recall data from 20+ national regulators every day. Each record is de-duplicated and normalised on the way in.

2 · Resolve to canonical identity

Brands, salt forms, synonyms and trade names roll up to a single INN via RxNorm and WHO ATC mappings — so LORSTAT and atorvastatin are one answer, not twelve.

3 · Layer live intelligence

Recalls link causally to shortages. Peer-set lead-time analysis across 16 EU markets surfaces forward signals. Pricing, alternatives and supplier data attach to every drug.

4 · Answer in plain language

A tool-using Claude agent queries the live database — 29k+ events and a 124-entry intelligence catalogue — and returns an operational answer with sources. Macro questions it can't ground, it declines rather than fabricates.

One query · one answer

"Is amoxicillin short, and what do I switch to?"

  • 17 active shortages resolved across markets
  • Canonical INN — every brand & salt rolled up
  • Alternatives ranked, with their own status
  • Linked recalls flagged as causal precursors
  • A grounded, sourced operational answer

Live today at mederti.vercel.app — not a mockup.

Persona-aware by design

The same data renders differently for each user. A pharmacist sees a radically simplified answer + actions card. Procurement sees supply-risk and sourcing. Suppliers see demand gaps and market opportunities. One backend, four products.
